How Much Does a Bathroom Remodel Cost in Everett, WA? (2026)

Published February 2026 by NW General Contractor

Bathroom Remodel Cost Overview in Everett

Bathroom remodeling in the Everett area typically costs between $6,000 and $35,000, depending on the scope of work, materials chosen, and the size of your bathroom. Snohomish County labor rates and material costs factor into these numbers. Here's a detailed breakdown to help you plan your budget.

Budget Bathroom Remodel ($6,000 - $12,000)

A budget remodel focuses on cosmetic updates that refresh the look without moving plumbing or changing the layout. This includes:

  • New vanity and sink ($300-$1,200)
  • Updated faucets and fixtures ($200-$600)
  • Fresh paint and updated lighting ($200-$500)
  • New toilet ($200-$600)
  • Re-grouting or painting existing tile ($300-$800)
  • New mirror and accessories ($100-$400)
This level works well for rental properties, quick refreshes before selling, or bathrooms that are structurally sound but visually dated.

Mid-Range Bathroom Remodel ($12,000 - $25,000)

A mid-range remodel is the most popular option among Everett homeowners. It typically includes:

  • New tile flooring and shower surround ($2,000-$5,000)
  • Custom vanity with quartz or granite countertop ($1,500-$3,500)
  • Tub-to-shower conversion or new shower enclosure ($2,500-$6,000)
  • Updated plumbing fixtures throughout ($500-$1,500)
  • Improved ventilation fan ($200-$600)
  • New lighting plan with recessed and vanity lights ($500-$1,200)
  • Heated tile floor ($500-$1,200)
According to Remodeling Magazine's Cost vs. Value report, a mid-range bathroom remodel recoups approximately 70.1% of its cost at resale — making it one of the best ROI projects you can do.

High-End Bathroom Remodel ($25,000 - $35,000+)

A high-end remodel transforms your bathroom into a spa-like retreat:

  • Custom tile work with designer patterns ($4,000-$8,000)
  • Walk-in shower with frameless glass, rain showerhead, body jets ($5,000-$10,000)
  • Freestanding soaker tub ($1,500-$4,000)
  • Custom cabinetry with soft-close drawers ($2,000-$5,000)
  • Quartz or marble countertops ($1,000-$3,000)
  • Radiant floor heating ($1,000-$2,500)
  • Smart features: heated toilet seat, motion-sensor faucets ($500-$2,000)

Cost Breakdown by Component

  • Labor: 40-50% of total ($2,400-$17,500) — Snohomish County rates
  • Tile and flooring: 15-20% ($900-$7,000)
  • Vanity and countertop: 10-15% ($600-$5,250)
  • Fixtures and hardware: 10-15% ($600-$5,250)
  • Plumbing: 5-10% ($300-$3,500)
  • Electrical: 5-8% ($300-$2,800)
Always add a 10-20% contingency for unexpected issues — especially in older Everett homes where hidden water damage or outdated plumbing can be discovered during demolition.

Factors That Affect Cost in the Everett Area

  • Home age: Pre-1978 homes may need lead paint remediation. Homes built in the 1970s-1990s (common in Everett) often have galvanized pipes that should be replaced.
  • Permits: Snohomish County requires permits for plumbing and electrical changes. Budget $200-$500 for permit fees.
  • Scope creep: Moving plumbing or walls increases cost significantly (add $3,000-$8,000).
  • Material choices: Porcelain tile vs. natural stone can swing costs by $2,000-$5,000.
  • Accessibility: ADA-compliant features (grab bars, curbless shower, wider doorway) add $1,000-$3,000 but increase accessibility and resale value.

Timeline: How Long Does a Bathroom Remodel Take?

In the Everett area, expect:

  • Budget remodel: 1-2 weeks
  • Mid-range remodel: 2-4 weeks
  • High-end remodel: 4-6 weeks
Timeline can extend if permits are required, custom materials need to be ordered, or if unexpected issues are found during demolition. The best approach is to have your contractor provide a detailed timeline before work begins.

Tips to Save on Your Bathroom Remodel

  1. Keep the existing layout — Moving plumbing is the single biggest cost driver. Keeping fixtures in the same locations saves thousands.
  2. Refinish instead of replace — Bathtub refinishing costs $300-$600 vs. $1,500-$4,000 for replacement.
  3. Choose porcelain over natural stone — Modern porcelain tile mimics marble and slate at a fraction of the cost.
  4. Do selective updates — Sometimes new fixtures, paint, and a new vanity are enough to transform the space.
  5. Get multiple quotes — Always get at least 3 written estimates from licensed contractors.
